Case in Action


Situation

When I joined a food packaging company a few years ago, I discovered the brand lacked foundational elements like a color scheme, visual identity, brand story, or mission. Customer perception was tied entirely to being "the cheaper option," which was unsustainable for long-term growth in a competitive market.

Objectives

My goal was to establish a cohesive brand identity that repositioned the company as a leader in eco-packaging and professionalism, rather than simply affordability. Key objectives included creating a strong visual identity, crafting a compelling brand story, and shifting customer perceptions through research-backed initiatives and improved communication.

Actions Taken

I led a comprehensive branding overhaul, including stakeholder communication, proposals, graphic design, copywriting, persona building, and market research. To overcome internal resistance to moving beyond the “cheap price” narrative, I presented case studies and data that highlighted the long-term benefits of emphasizing quality and professionalism. Once the brand transformation was implemented, we re-engaged with clients to measure its impact through follow-ups and surveys.

Results

The results were transformative. Client retention improved by 20% within one year, and customer ratings jumped from 3.8 to 4.8 out of 5. Customers reported that even with prices at or above the industry average, they stuck with us due to our promises of better service, faster processing, high-quality packaging, and a wider range of options. The brand is now associated with eco-packaging and professionalism, proving the power of strategic branding to drive both loyalty and business growth.
